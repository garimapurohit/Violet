Hormones & Your Body

Proper hygiene reduces the risk of infections like UTIs, bacterial vaginosis, and yeast infections. It also ensures comfort and boosts overall reproductive wellness.

Daily Hygiene Essentials

  • Wash your intimate area with water (avoid harsh soaps)
  • Always wipe front to back
  • Change underwear daily (prefer cotton)
  • Stay dry—moisture invites bacteria

Menstrual Hygiene Tips

  • Change pads/tampons every 4–6 hours
  • Don’t use scented products internally
  • Wash hands before and after changing products
  • Clean your menstrual cup properly after each cycle

When to See a Doctor

If you experience unusual discharge, itching, foul odor, or pain—don’t ignore it. These might signal infections that need professional care.
