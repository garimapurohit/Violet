Hormones & Your Body

Decode your cycle! From phases to flow and what different blood colors mean—understand what’s normal and what deserves attention.

The 4 Phases of the Cycle

  • Menstrual: Shedding of uterine lining (bleeding phase)
  • Follicular: Hormones prepare the egg
  • Ovulation: Egg is released (mid-cycle)
  • Luteal: Hormones prep for pregnancy or restart cycle

What Your Blood Color Can Tell You

  • Bright Red: Fresh blood, healthy flow
  • Dark Brown: Older blood from earlier in the cycle (normal at start or end)
  • Pink: Low estrogen or spotting (check if frequent)
  • Gray: Sign of infection—see a doctor
  • Orange: Possible infection if accompanied by odor or discomfort

When to See a Doctor

Extremely painful periods, heavy bleeding (changing pads hourly), missed periods, or irregular cycles could be signs of PCOS, fibroids, or thyroid issues.
